AI Risk Summary

**TARC Limited – Financial Risk Summary** TARC's primary financial exposure stems from critical compliance gaps: a perfect 10.0/10 compliance risk score combined with unknown EPR (Extended Producer Responsibility) applicability creates regulatory enforcement vulnerability under SEBI BRSR Core Expansion, with potential penalties and reputational costs that could impact its modest ₹38.9 crore revenue base. The company faces mandatory disclosure obligations under BRSR P6 environmental standards, yet reports zero Scope 1 and 2 emissions data—likely indicating inadequate measurement infrastructure rather than genuine zero emissions, exposing it to SEBI enforcement action and possible delisting risk if classified as top-tier. EPR exposure (6.5/10 risk score) represents a secondary but unquantified financial threat; without clarity on whether residential construction materials fall under EPR mandates, TARC risks unexpected compliance costs and extended liability for product end-of-life management. Immediate priority: conduct regulatory impact assessment for EPR classification, establish robust emissions measurement protocols aligned with BRSR Core requirements, and remediate compliance documentation to mitigate enforcement risk.
