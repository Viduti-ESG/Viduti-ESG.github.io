AI Risk Summary

**Financial Risk Summary – Wheels India Limited** Wheels India faces its most acute financial exposure through regulatory non-compliance, with a critical 10.0/10 compliance risk score and estimated remediation costs of ₹7–26 crore—representing up to 0.59% of annual revenue. The company's undefined EPR (Extended Producer Responsibility) applicability status creates contingent liability uncertainty under SEBI BRSR Core Expansion mandates, with potential enforcement actions and delisting risk for failure to meet disclosure obligations. Medium-level GHG and water intensity risks (5.0/10 each) indicate emerging carbon pricing exposure; if Scope 1–2 emissions data gaps are resolved, the company could face ₹30–45 crore in annual carbon costs under India's Carbon Credit Trading Scheme at ₹600–900/tonne. Immediate priority: clarify EPR applicability, resolve emissions accounting gaps, and establish BRSR compliance governance to mitigate regulatory and capital market penalties.
