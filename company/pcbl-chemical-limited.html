Bottlenecks & Best-Fit Global Solutions

Material ESG issues PCBL CHEMICAL LIMITED disclosed in its BRSR, each mapped to the globally recognised standard or playbook best suited to address it. Solution mappings are Green Curve's curated guidance based on published international frameworks — not endorsed by the issuing bodies.

Risk GHG emissions and energy management ◆ Positive Implications

Best-fit global solution: Adopt an ISO 50001 energy-management system and pursue certified efficiency gains; in India, align to BEE's PAT (Perform-Achieve-Trade) scheme for ESCert value.

ISO 50001BEE PAT scheme
Reference: ISO 50001 Energy management ↗ · BEE PAT Scheme ↗
What PCBL CHEMICAL LIMITED says it is doing: Accounting/monitoring GHG emissions at regular intervals. Long term/short term corrective measures to improve energy efficiency by using heat exchangers at different stages of downstream and thereby reducing the combustion of fossil fuel. Generation of electrical power by using waste gases (tail gas) released during carbon black production process, and excess electricity, after meeting requirements, is exported to grid and thereby offsetting the GHG emission which would have been emitted otherwise by using fossil fuel.
Risk Water management ◆ Positive Implications

Best-fit global solution: Commit to the UN CEO Water Mandate and certify sites to the Alliance for Water Stewardship (AWS) Standard; deploy Zero Liquid Discharge (ZLD) and closed-loop recycling in water-stressed basins.

CEO Water MandateAWS StandardZero Liquid Discharge
Reference: UN CEO Water Mandate ↗ · Alliance for Water Stewardship ↗
What PCBL CHEMICAL LIMITED says it is doing: Monitoring water consumption in various processes at the operational level. Exploring water-recycling opportunities to save freshwater consumption. Replenishing groundwater through the utilisation of harvested rainwater for horticulture. All our plants are zero liquid discharge (ZLD) compliant.
Risk Solid waste management ◆ Positive Implications

Best-fit global solution: Apply Ellen MacArthur Foundation circular-economy design, target Zero Waste to Landfill (UL 2799 validation), and meet India's Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) obligations for plastic/e-waste/battery.

Ellen MacArthur CEUL 2799 Zero Waste to LandfillIndia EPR
Reference: Ellen MacArthur Foundation ↗ · CPCB EPR (India) ↗
What PCBL CHEMICAL LIMITED says it is doing: Monitor and map the quantity of different types of wastes generated - Reduce the quantity of waste generated at source by adopting adequate measures - Explore possibilities for recycling and reusing the waste generated - Ensure waste generated is disposed-off in an appropriate manner. - Increasing the percentage of waste diversion from landfill
Risk Product stewardship ◆ Positive Implications

Best-fit global solution: Quantify product footprint with ISO 14040/14044 Life Cycle Assessment, pursue credible eco-labels (Type I / EPD), and design for durability and recyclability.

ISO 14040/44 LCAEnvironmental Product DeclarationEco-design
Reference: ISO 14040 LCA ↗ · EPD International ↗
What PCBL CHEMICAL LIMITED says it is doing: Initiative for Life cycle assessment of the product and taking action to reduce the impact of its product on the environment. - Adherence to applicable national and international regulations (e.g., REACH, ROHS), emphasising continuously on updating domain knowledge through R&D, analysing and highlighting implications and staying ahead of the compliance curve.
Risk Health and safety ◆ Positive Implications

Best-fit global solution: Certify to ISO 45001 and drive an interdependent safety culture using the DuPont Bradley Curve and behaviour-based safety; track leading indicators, not just LTIFR.

ISO 45001DuPont Bradley CurveBehaviour-Based Safety
Reference: ISO 45001 OH&S ↗
What PCBL CHEMICAL LIMITED says it is doing: Emphasis on Hazard Identification and Risk Assessment, HAZOP (Hazard and Operability Study), and JSA (Job Safety Analysis), incident investigation and training - Occupational health and safety management programme across all plants. - Plants certified with ISO 45001:2018 standard. - Established Safety Committee across all plants, which consists of Unit Head (Chairperson), Safety Head (Secretary), all department heads and contract worker representatives representing each contractor. - Strict adherence to SOPs for following the highest level of health and hygiene.
Opportunity Human rights and employee wellbeing ◆ Negative Implications

Best-fit global solution: Operationalise the UN Guiding Principles on Business & Human Rights (UNGPs) via human-rights due diligence and grievance mechanisms; certify labour sites to SA8000.

UN Guiding Principles (UNGPs)SA8000HR Due Diligence
Reference: UN Guiding Principles ↗ · SA8000 Standard ↗
What PCBL CHEMICAL LIMITED says it is doing: Non-acceptance of (Zerotolerance approach towards) human rights violations such as discrimination, forced/coercive labour, and child labour within and beyond PCBL’s boundaries. - Implementation of a welldefined Human Rights Policy, with awareness training provided to the entire workforce. - The Human Rights Policy outlines PCBL’s approach and expectations for human rights compliance throughout the value chain. - Adherence to the human rights policy is closely monitored, and a whistleblower process is in place for stakeholders to raise concerns.
