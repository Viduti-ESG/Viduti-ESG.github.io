AI Risk Summary

**V-MART RETAIL LIMITED – FINANCIAL RISK SUMMARY** V-MART faces critical compliance exposure of ₹5–20 crore annually, primarily driven by uncertain Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) applicability in apparel and mandatory BRSR disclosures under SEBI Core Expansion rules; non-compliance risks include enforcement action and potential delisting for top-tier companies. The company's complete absence of reported Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions data (despite ₹3,253.9 crore revenue) signals either measurement gaps or non-disclosure, creating regulatory vulnerability under BRSR P6 environmental disclosure requirements. EPR exposure rated 6.5/10 indicates material financial risk from producer liability obligations if V-MART is classified as an extended producer under India's plastic waste management rules, potentially requiring upstream waste collection and processing infrastructure investment. The combination of 10.0/10 compliance risk rating and medium ESG score suggests immediate need for robust emissions accounting and regulatory clarity on EPR applicability to mitigate enforcement penalties and stakeholder capital access risks.
