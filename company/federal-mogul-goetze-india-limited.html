AI Risk Summary

**Financial Risk Summary: Federal-Mogul Goetze (India) Limited** Federal-Mogul Goetze faces its largest financial exposure through compliance risk (10.0/10 score) with estimated remediation costs of ₹283–1,062 crore, primarily driven by uncertain EPR (Extended Producer Responsibility) applicability and incomplete emissions reporting under SEBI BRSR Core Expansion mandates. The company's critical regulatory obligation is mandatory BRSR P6 environmental disclosures and potential EPR compliance for automotive components; non-compliance carries delisting risk for top-tier listed entities and enforcement action from SEBI. With Scope 1 and 2 emissions unreported despite ₹176,929 crore in revenue, the company faces immediate audit exposure—at India's carbon credit benchmark of ₹600–900/tonne, unaccounted emissions could trigger significant financial liability once quantified. Priority action: urgently establish baseline GHG inventory and clarify EPR regulatory status to mitigate the wide ₹779 crore cost uncertainty band.
