AI Risk Summary

**Financial Risk Summary: Unimech Aerospace and Manufacturing Limited** Unimech's largest financial exposure stems from its 6.5/10 EPR (Extended Producer Responsibility) exposure, particularly under India's plastic and e-waste management rules, where compliance failures can trigger penalties up to ₹5 lakh per violation plus product recall costs—a material risk for a ₹42.4 crore revenue company. The company faces elevated compliance risk (7.8/10) under SEBI BRSR Core Expansion requirements; failure to establish mandatory ESG governance structures and report verified emissions data could result in enforcement action and potential exchange delisting. Critical gap: zero reported Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions despite aerospace manufacturing typically requiring significant energy consumption, suggesting either non-compliance with BRSR P6 disclosure rules or genuine underreporting that regulators will likely scrutinize, with potential reputational and regulatory costs ranging ₹20–50 lakh in audit remediation and penalties if data integrity is questioned.
