AI Risk Summary

**Financial Risk Summary – Inox Wind Limited** Inox Wind faces its most acute financial exposure through compliance risk (10/10 score), with estimated remediation costs of ₹6–21 crore under SEBI BRSR Core Expansion mandates; non-compliance could trigger enforcement action or delisting for listed entities. Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) exposure remains undefined and material—the 6.5/10 EPR risk score suggests unquantified liabilities for wind turbine component recycling under India's waste regulations, particularly as sector-specific EPR rules crystallize. The company's reported zero Scope 1 and 2 emissions data raises credibility concerns and suggests incomplete environmental accounting rather than genuine zero impact, creating regulatory scrutiny risk alongside potential carbon pricing exposure of ₹600–900/tonne under the India Carbon Credit Trading System (CCTS) once emissions are properly disclosed. Immediate priorities: clarify EPR obligations under CIMS/e-waste rules, complete emissions inventory, and close BRSR disclosure gaps to mitigate delisting and enforcement risks.
