Risk & Opportunity Climate and Energy ◆ Negative Implications

Best-fit global solution: Set a Science Based Target (SBTi), measure with the GHG Protocol, and decarbonise via RE100-style renewable sourcing and TCFD/IFRS-S2 climate-risk scenario analysis.

SBTiGHG ProtocolRE100TCFD / IFRS S2
Reference: Science Based Targets initiative ↗ · GHG Protocol ↗ · RE100 ↗
What Sanghi Industries Limited says it is doing: The Company has approximately 35% of products in its portfolio which are blended products with lower carbon footprint. Further, we are investing more and more in renewal energy and green energy from WHRS. In addition, we have set ambitious targets for Thermal Substitution Rates (TSR) by using alternate fuels.
Risk Air quality ◆ Negative Implications

Best-fit global solution: Install Continuous Emission Monitoring Systems (CEMS) and abatement to CPCB norms; manage under an ISO 14001 environmental-management system.

CPCB CEMS normsISO 14001
Reference: CPCB Emission Standards ↗ · ISO 14001 ↗
What Sanghi Industries Limited says it is doing: We focus on improving air quality in the plant & surrounding area. We monitor the plants’ stack emissions through the Continuous Emission Monitoring System. We work on upgradation of electrostatic precipitators and replacement of damaged bags to control dust emissions. We take primary and secondary measures to control NOx emissions.
Risk & Opportunity Water management ◆ Negative Implications

Best-fit global solution: Commit to the UN CEO Water Mandate and certify sites to the Alliance for Water Stewardship (AWS) Standard; deploy Zero Liquid Discharge (ZLD) and closed-loop recycling in water-stressed basins.

CEO Water MandateAWS StandardZero Liquid Discharge
Reference: UN CEO Water Mandate ↗ · Alliance for Water Stewardship ↗
What Sanghi Industries Limited says it is doing: The company has long been dedicated to mitigating water scarcity through various initiatives, including rainwater harvesting, restoring village ponds, constructing check dams, conserving water at closed mines, and recharging groundwater. The company withdraws only harvested water for its usage and uses desalinated water solely for cooling purposes. Water used in the plant for industrial cooling is recycled through cooling towers to ensure effective reuse.
