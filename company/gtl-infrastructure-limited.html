AI Risk Summary

**GTL Infrastructure Limited – Financial Risk Summary** GTL Infrastructure faces critical compliance exposure with a 10.0/10 compliance risk score and estimated remediation costs of ₹2–8 crore under SEBI BRSR Core Expansion mandates; non-compliance risks include potential enforcement action and delisting for top-tier listed entities. The company's highest financial vulnerability lies in Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) obligations with a 6.5/10 exposure rating—telecom infrastructure generates significant electronic waste through tower equipment and power systems, creating undefined liability until EPR scope clarification. While Scope 1 and 2 emissions are reported as zero (likely incomplete disclosure rather than genuine absence for a passive infrastructure operator with diesel backup power and grid consumption), the company must quantify and disclose emissions under BRSR P6 environmental standards or face regulatory penalties and potential carbon pricing exposure worth ₹600–900/tonne if India's Carbon Credit Trading Scheme applies retroactively. Immediate priority: complete BRSR Part 7 compliance audit and establish EPR framework for telecom waste streams to mitigate ₹2–8
