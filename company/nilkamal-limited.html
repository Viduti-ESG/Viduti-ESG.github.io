AI Risk Summary

**Nilkamal Limited – Financial Risk Summary** Nilkamal faces critical compliance exposure with a maximum 10.0/10 risk rating and estimated remediation costs of ₹5–19 crore, primarily driven by unclear Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) obligations under plastic waste management rules—a material liability for a plastics manufacturer with ₹3,239 crore revenue. The company's failure to report Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions data creates immediate SEBI BRSR non-compliance risk, potentially triggering enforcement action and delisting consequences if addressing BRSR Core Expansion disclosure requirements. EPR exposure (6.5/10 risk) represents the largest unquantified financial exposure; absent clarity on applicable waste collection and recycling targets, contingent liabilities could escalate significantly beyond the ₹5–19 crore estimate if regulatory interpretation becomes stricter or retroactive compliance is mandated. Urgent priority: establish verified emissions accounting, confirm EPR applicability scope, and quantify waste management obligations to prevent regulatory penalties and institutional investor capital restriction.
