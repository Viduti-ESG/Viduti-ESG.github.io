What is the EPR market? Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) makes producers, importers and brand owners (PIBOs) responsible for recycling the packaging and products they place on the market. They meet annual targets by procuring EPR certificates from CPCB-registered recyclers — certificates that are category-specific and traded on CPCB portals and private exchanges. The market was valued at roughly $1.5 billion in 2025 and is projected to reach $5 billion by 2030. New to it? Read the full guide: The EPR Certificate Market in India (2026) →

Targets are the recycling/collection obligation as a share of quantity placed on market. Price bands are indicative 2025 trading ranges, not official quotes.

How the cost works. Obligation (t) = quantity placed on market × target%. Procurement cost = obligation shortfall × certificate price. Environmental Compensation (EC) is what CPCB levies on the unmet portion — designed to be higher than the cost of genuine compliance, so buying certificates is almost always cheaper than defaulting.

Seasonality: prices spike Jan–Mar. Demand for certificates concentrates near the annual return deadline, so rates typically climb sharply in Q4 (Jan–Mar). PIBOs that procure early in the year usually pay materially less than last-minute buyers.

2026 Rule Change

Recycled-Content Planner

The Plastic Waste Management (Amendment) Rules, 2026 make recycled content mandatory and rising — rigid plastic must hit 60% recycled material by FY2028-29. Plan the recycled tonnage you'll need to source.

Also new in 2026: End-of-Life (EOL) certificates can no longer be used to meet recycling targets, and FY2025-26 shortfalls can be carried forward up to three years if at least one-third of the deficit is cleared annually.

Recycled-content percentages are indicative, based on the Plastic Waste Management (Amendment) Rules, 2026. Verify exact figures and category definitions against the gazette notification before acting.
