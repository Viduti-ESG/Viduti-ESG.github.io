BRSR Reporting in India: The Complete Guide

The Business Responsibility and Sustainability Report (BRSR) is SEBI's mandatory ESG disclosure for India's largest listed companies. This guide explains what BRSR is, who must file it, what goes in each section, and how the BRSR Core assurance mandate ramps up to the top 1,000 companies by FY 2026-27.

What is BRSR?

BRSR (Business Responsibility and Sustainability Report) is a standardised ESG disclosure framework introduced by the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) in May 2021. It replaced the older Business Responsibility Report (BRR) and is built on the nine principles of the National Guidelines on Responsible Business Conduct (NGRBC).

BRSR converts sustainability from a narrative annexure into a structured, quantitative, comparable disclosure — covering greenhouse-gas emissions, energy and water use, waste, employee wellbeing, human rights, community impact and governance — filed as part of the company's annual report.

Who must file BRSR? (Applicability)

BRSR is mandatory for the top 1,000 listed companies by market capitalisation (as of 31 March each year) from FY 2022-23 onwards. Companies below that threshold may file voluntarily, and unlisted companies increasingly adopt it for value-chain and investor reasons.

Tip: Market cap is assessed each 31 March, so a company that crosses into the top 1,000 becomes subject to BRSR for that financial year. Track your rank early.

BRSR format: the three sections

The BRSR is organised into three sections:

SectionWhat it covers
A — General DisclosuresCompany identity, operations, products, employees, CSR, complaints.
B — Management & ProcessPolicies, governance, leadership and oversight mapped to the 9 NGRBC principles.
C — Principle-wise PerformanceQuantitative and qualitative KPIs for each of the 9 principles — the bulk of the report.

Disclosures are split into Essential Indicators (mandatory for all filers) and Leadership Indicators (advanced metrics, voluntary but expected of mature programmes) — roughly 140 data points in total.

The 9 NGRBC principles

  1. P1 — Ethics & transparency: integrity, anti-corruption, governance.
  2. P2 — Sustainable & safe goods: product lifecycle, R&D, recyclability.
  3. P3 — Employee wellbeing: wages, safety, benefits, diversity.
  4. P4 — Stakeholder responsiveness: identifying and engaging stakeholders.
  5. P5 — Human rights: across operations and the value chain.
  6. P6 — Environment: emissions, energy, water, waste, biodiversity.
  7. P7 — Policy advocacy: responsible and transparent lobbying.
  8. P8 — Inclusive growth: community development and equitable impact.
  9. P9 — Consumer value: product safety, data privacy, fair disclosure.

BRSR Core and the assurance glide-path

BRSR Core is a focused subset of the most decision-useful ESG KPIs (around 49 attributes across nine themes such as GHG intensity, water, energy, waste, and job creation) that require independent reasonable assurance. SEBI introduced it in July 2023 with a phased rollout:

Financial yearReasonable assurance on BRSR Core
FY 2023-24Top 150 listed companies
FY 2024-25Top 250 listed companies
FY 2025-26Top 500 listed companies
FY 2026-27Top 1,000 listed companies

A parallel value-chain requirement extends ESG disclosure (on a comply-or-explain basis) to the company's significant upstream and downstream partners — making supplier data collection a practical necessity.

How to prepare your BRSR

A practical sequence:

  1. Map data owners across HR, EHS, finance, procurement and legal.
  2. Build your GHG inventory (Scope 1, 2 and material Scope 3) using consistent emission factors.
  3. Collect value-chain data from significant suppliers early — it is the slowest input.
  4. Draft Section C principle-by-principle, keeping audit-ready evidence.
  5. Get BRSR Core assured if you are in scope for the year.
