Scope 3 Emissions: The Value-Chain Guide

Scope 3 covers the indirect emissions across a company's value chain — usually the largest and hardest-to-measure part of the carbon footprint. This guide explains the 15 GHG Protocol categories, why Scope 3 matters for BRSR and SBTi, and a practical path to measuring it.

Scope 1, 2 and 3 — the difference

The GHG Protocol splits emissions into three scopes:

  • Scope 1 — direct emissions from owned or controlled sources (fuel combustion, company vehicles, process emissions).
  • Scope 2 — indirect emissions from purchased electricity, steam, heat and cooling.
  • Scope 3 — all other indirect emissions across the value chain, upstream and downstream.
Why it matters: For most companies Scope 3 is 70%+ of total emissions — sometimes well over 90% — so a footprint without it is incomplete.

The 15 Scope 3 categories

The GHG Protocol Corporate Value Chain (Scope 3) Standard defines 15 categories — 8 upstream and 7 downstream:

1. Purchased goods & services9. Downstream transport & distribution
2. Capital goods10. Processing of sold products
3. Fuel- & energy-related activities11. Use of sold products
4. Upstream transport & distribution12. End-of-life of sold products
5. Waste generated in operations13. Downstream leased assets
6. Business travel14. Franchises
7. Employee commuting15. Investments
8. Upstream leased assets

How to measure Scope 3

A pragmatic approach:

  1. Screen all 15 categories to find which are material to your business — typically categories 1, 4, 11 and 12.
  2. Start spend-based (emission factors × procurement spend) for a fast first estimate.
  3. Move to activity- and supplier-specific data for your largest categories over time.
  4. Engage suppliers for primary data — the single biggest accuracy improvement.

Scope 3 for BRSR and SBTi in India

India's BRSR captures Scope 1 and 2 as essential indicators and Scope 3 as a leadership indicator, while BRSR Core and value-chain disclosures push Scope 3 up the agenda. For science-based targets, the SBTi requires a Scope 3 inventory and target where Scope 3 exceeds 40% of total emissions — true for most companies.
