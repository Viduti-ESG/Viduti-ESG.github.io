Questions & Answers

Carbon footprint & GHG calculator — FAQ

Is this GHG emissions calculator really free?

Yes. Green Curve's GHG and carbon footprint calculator is free to use, with no sign-up required to estimate your Scope 1, 2 and 3 emissions. Create a free account only if you want to save calculations or generate a BRSR-ready Scope 1/2/3 report.

How do I calculate Scope 1, 2 and 3 emissions for a company in India?

Pick an emission scope, add each activity (fuel burnt, electricity purchased, business travel, purchased goods, etc.), and the calculator multiplies your activity data by the matching emission factor to give tonnes of CO₂e. Scope 1 is direct combustion, Scope 2 is purchased electricity (using India's CEA grid factor), and Scope 3 covers value-chain emissions. See our full Scope 3 emissions guide for the 15 categories.

Which emission factors does the carbon footprint calculator use?

It uses 2,700+ emission factors from the UK Government GHG Conversion Factors (DEFRA, under the Open Government Licence), the IFI Default Grid Factors dataset, and India's Central Electricity Authority (CEA) CO₂ Baseline V21.0 for grid electricity, applied per the GHG Protocol Corporate Standard. India-specific factors make the result accurate for Indian operations rather than relying on generic global averages.

Can I use this for SEBI BRSR or ISSB S2 reporting?

Yes. The calculator is aligned with the GHG Protocol, SEBI BRSR and ISSB IFRS S2 disclosure requirements, and can export a draft Scope 1/2/3 breakdown. Pair it with our BRSR reporting guide and BRSR report generator to move from emissions data to a filing-ready disclosure. Always verify the draft before filing.

How does my company compare with peers on emissions?

After calculating your footprint, see where you stand against 1,200+ Indian listed companies on the ESG Quotient dashboard, or browse ESG scores by sector to benchmark against your industry.
