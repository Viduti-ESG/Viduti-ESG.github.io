Green Curve Bookings — Terms & Privacy Notice

Version GC-BK-1.0  ·  Effective: 8 July 2026  ·  Governing law: India (DPDP Act 2023; IT Act 2000)  ·  GDPR-aligned

This notice covers Green Curve Bookings, the scheduling service at greencurve.solutions/bookings. It explains what data we handle when you book a meeting or run a booking room, the legal basis for it, and the rights available to you. By confirming a booking, you agree to the invitee terms in Part B and consent to the processing described in Part C.

1. Who is who

Green Curve Bookings lets a registered user (the "Host") create a private booking room and accept meeting bookings from members of the public ("Invitees"). Under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 ("DPDP Act") and, where relevant, the EU GDPR:

The Host = Data Fiduciary / Controller

The Host determines why and how invitee data for their room is used (to schedule and conduct meetings). The Host is responsible for having a lawful purpose and for honouring invitee requests.

Green Curve = Data Processor

Green Curve Solutions operates the platform and processes invitee data on behalf of the Host, strictly to provide the scheduling service. We do not sell invitee data or use it for advertising.

For a Host's own account data (name, email, login), Green Curve is the Data Fiduciary — see our main Privacy Policy.

2. Part A — Terms for Hosts

2.1 Your responsibilities

  • You will only collect invitee data for genuine scheduling purposes and will not use it for unsolicited marketing without a separate, lawful consent obtained by you.
  • You are responsible for the content of your room — its name, branding, descriptions and cancellation policies — and confirm you have the right to use any name or brand you enter (e.g. an academy, NGO or company name).
  • You will respond to invitee data requests that reach you, and will not obstruct the erasure and cancellation tools the platform provides to invitees.
  • You will not use Bookings for unlawful, harmful, deceptive, or infringing purposes, or to schedule activity that violates applicable law.

2.2 What Green Curve provides

  • Consent capture, timestamped and version-stamped, before any invitee data is stored.
  • Self-service invitee tools (cancel, erase) and a full data export for you (portability).
  • Automatic anonymisation of invitee personal data 180 days after a meeting ends.
  • A tracker-free public booking surface.
Green Curve acts as your processor. This notice, together with our Terms of Use, forms the data-processing arrangement between us for booking data. We process invitee data only on your documented instructions as expressed through your use of the product.

3. Part B — Terms for Invitees

  • A confirmed booking is a request to meet at the stated time; it is not a contract for any paid service unless separately agreed with the Host.
  • Please observe the Host's stated cancellation policy. You can cancel or reschedule using the secure link in your confirmation email.
  • The meeting itself (its content, conduct and any advice given) is between you and the Host. Green Curve only provides the scheduling tool.
  • Provide accurate contact details so the Host can reach you. Do not submit another person's data without their knowledge.

4.1 Who your data is shared with

  • The Host of the room you booked — they receive your name, email and notes to hold the meeting.
  • Email delivery — a transactional email provider is used solely to deliver your confirmation and calendar invite.
  • We do not sell your data or share it with advertisers or data brokers. Data is hosted on Green Curve infrastructure in India.

5. Your data rights

Under the DPDP Act 2023 (and the GDPR where it applies) you can:

  • Access & confirmation — see your booking details anytime via your manage link.
  • Correction — cancel and rebook, or ask the Host to correct details.
  • Erasure ("right to be forgotten") — permanently delete your booking and personal data yourself, using "Delete my data" on your manage link. No request or justification needed.
  • Portability (Hosts) — export a full machine-readable copy of your room and its bookings from the Booking Studio.
  • Withdraw consent — withdrawing consent is as easy as giving it; deleting your data withdraws it entirely.
  • Grievance & nomination — raise a concern with us (below) and, if unresolved, with the Data Protection Board of India.

6. Retention

Invitee personal data (name, email, notes) is automatically anonymised 180 days after the meeting ends, unless you delete it sooner. Anonymised, non-identifying records may be kept for the Host's aggregate history. If a Host deletes their room, all associated booking data — including invitee data — is permanently removed.

7. Security

  • Data in transit is encrypted over HTTPS.
  • Booking management and erasure use unguessable secure tokens rather than public identifiers.
  • Access to booking data requires the Host's authenticated account; personal data is not written to server logs.
  • Rate limiting and a spam honeypot protect the booking surface from abuse.

8. Liability

Green Curve Bookings is provided as an automated scheduling utility "as is". To the maximum extent permitted by law, Green Curve is not liable for missed meetings, scheduling errors, a Host's conduct, or the outcome of any meeting arranged through the tool. Green Curve's total aggregate liability arising from the scheduling service is limited to correcting the affected booking. Nothing here excludes liability that cannot be excluded under applicable law.

9. Grievance redressal

For any privacy question or complaint about Green Curve Bookings, contact our Grievance Officer:

  • Email: privacy@greencurve.solutions
  • Entity: Green Curve Solutions, India

We aim to acknowledge grievances within 72 hours. If a matter concerns data collected by a specific Host, we will help route it to that Host as the responsible Data Fiduciary. Unresolved complaints may be escalated to the Data Protection Board of India.
